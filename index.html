Background

My first was in finance (my degrees majored in Finance and accounting), analysing and trading FX futures, in a role that would now be considered a ‘Quant’. I saw the opportunity to join up web pages (as a presentation layer), with data sources like pricing tables for flights and transactional platforms such as airline reservation systems to build out new online business solutions - Online travel agency or retailers selling products, much in the same way I used to be able to currencies on my Fixed Line trading platform previously. Rather than building my own tech, I looked into what was available, and that led me to Intershop, Europe’s leading ecommerce platform at the time. I joined the business in 1997, as THE technical resource for the UK. This mostly focused on Solution Consulting, but in the rapid growth of the Dotcom boom, I grew my team to 50, looking after Pre-sales, Professional Services, Training, Customer Support and IT. I continued being the pre-sales lead for the strategic opportunities in the UK until I left in 2001, spending a few years doing the same sort of role for an eGovernment platform. In 2005, I joined Scene7, as the first hire outside the US, to support the VP Sales expand into the EMEA region. I performed mostly as a very commercial pre-sales leader for the business, even after the acquisition by Adobe, leading to being the Pre-sales person for the top global sales person in 2008 achieving 650% of quota. This prompted my move to a Sales role in 2009. After significant success, I wanted to ensure that a lot of that was due to my capabilities, not just the Adobe name. In 2012, I joined a tiny start up, with minimal license revenue and helped to transform that business, contributing to over 100x increase in recurring revenue over 12 years. I put down my sales success to listening and understanding customer needs, being empathetic and constructing solutions that will deliver value to them.
